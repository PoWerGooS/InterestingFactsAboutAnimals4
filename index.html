Camel

  1. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.
  2. Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months!
  3. When a camel finally does find water, he can drink up to 40 gallons in one go.

Pandas

  • Pandas spend most of their day finding and eating food.
  • Panda infants are one of the smallest mammal infants when they are born.
  • There are approximately only 1,864 pandas in the wild.

Pigeons

  1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals
  2. Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities
  3. Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.

Chameleons

  • Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder.
  • Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chameleons.
  • Most chameleons have prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches.

Flying Cicadas

  1. Flying Cicadas can survive huge falls as babies.
  2. Male Flying Cicadas can produce up to sounds that are 120 decibels which is among the loudest for insects.
  3. The lifespan of a Flying Cicada is between 4-17 years.

Platypus

  • The platypus is only one of 2 egg-laying mammals.
  • The platypus is nocturnal which means it spends most of it's time at night.
  • The platypus is one of only three mammals that produces venom.

Rhinos

  • There are 5 different types of species of rhinos
  • Rhinos have very poor vision so they rely on their smell.
  • The Javan Rhino is the most endangered animal in the world behind the Vaquita.

Seal

  1. The largest seal wieghs over 4 tons
  2. They can hold their breath for more than 100 minutes, which is longer than any other non-cetacean mammal.
  3. Seals spend up to 80% of their lives in the ocean.
